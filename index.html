ORIGINS

Sun Ra, born Herman Poole Blount (1914-1993) was a Jazz composer and Musician, creator of the Sun Ra Arkestra. He pioneered Afrofuturism and contriubted to the Jazz Genre. Despite his great accolades, Sun Ra was born to simple means.

1914

Sun Ra was born in 1914 in Birmingham, AL. A talented musician from a young age, Sun Ra learned to play the piano and composed music. He gained experience with bands in his early years

1945

After WWII, Sun Ra moved to Chicago to commence his wider musical career. Here, he achieved greater success and led many bands, leading to the formation of his own group, the Arkestra

Finish the Journey

Sun Ra moved from Chicago to Philadelphia in the 1960s. Starting in 1968, Sun Ra and his band of 20-30 members began touring internationally, with elaborate performances. In this period, Sun Ra began to truly express his spiritual interests.

The outfits and performances of the band invoked Egyptian and African mythology, and Sun Ra read heavily from ancient texts and spiritualist literature from a number of backgrounds and faiths. This cosmic aesthetic defined the group.

Achievements

Sun Ra led a truly prolific career. He released and contributed to over 100 studio albums and created music for over five decades. The impact of his stellar work on the world of Jazz and music was immense by his death in the year 1993.
